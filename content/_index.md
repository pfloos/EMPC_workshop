# Embedding Theory: Bridging Math, Physics, and Chemistry

Welcome to the [NanoX](https://nanox-toulouse.fr) Workshop on Embedding in Mathematics, Physics, and Chemistry (EMPC), dedicated to the rapidly evolving field of quantum embedding. This theoretical paradigm enables accurate simulation of complex materials and molecules that challenge traditional methods by dividing systems into fragments treated with high-level theories, while interactions between fragments are captured through lower-level models. The local and non-local descriptions of the system communicate through a self-consistent closure of the embedding equations. Quantum embedding has the potential to transform the study of diverse systems, from transition metal catalysts to advanced materials, and continues to inspire mathematicians, physicists, and chemists alike. Yet, interdisciplinary dialogue remains rare, despite the method’s universal applicability and shared formalism across fields. Over three days, this workshop will bring together experts from these three fields to exchange ideas, explore the theoretical and practical challenges of embedding, and foster a collaborative community that bridges disciplinary boundaries to advance electronic structure research.
